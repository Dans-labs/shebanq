{{=T('Get started')}}

Just have a look at the {{=A(T("shibboleth query"), _href=URL('mql', 'display_query', vars=dict(id=18)))}} (1 result) or the {{=A(T("definite article query"), _href=URL('mql', 'display_query', vars=dict(id=13)))}} (more than 10,000 results) or find an other {{=A(T("public query"), _href=URL('mql', 'public_queries'))}} that interests you.
Try to click on query names, query authors and passage headings. In this way you switch back and forth from browsing passages to inspecting relevant queries. Or start reading a {{=A(T("passage"), _href=URL('passage', 'browser', vars=dict(book='Genesis', chapter=1, qget='v')))}} and look which queries land there. Do not forget to check the underlying data. You can customize the data that is shown, and lookup the meaning of the data fields by clicking on it in the legend.

{{=T('Next step: make your own queries')}}

You can contribute queries yourself, but then you have to register/login. By filling in your institution and your project you provide other users with a bit of context.
Start with the menu item {{=A(T("My Queries"), _href=URL('mql', 'my_queries'))}}. You can add a query by means of the + button, and you can edit, save, execute existing queries.
Precise, concise documentation is at your finger tips: {{=A('MQL quick reference guide', _target='_blank',_href=URL('static', 'docs/MQL-QuickRef.pdf'))}} and {{=A('ETCBC4 transcription table', _target='_blank',_href=URL('static', 'docs/ETCBC4-transcription.pdf'))}}.

There is a growing body of online documentation on readthedocs.

{{=T('Expert step: publish your queries')}}

You can publish your queries, by pressing the Publish button where you edit the query (you can also unpublish them by pressing Make Private).
When you press Publish the query will be executed first, in order to make sure that the query results match the query instruction.
Published queries show up under Public Queries. They also are shown in the margin when you browse Bible chapters containing their results.
In this way they are become a valuable resource for fellow Hebrew readers.
