{{=T('About')}}

The shebanq service is the first data service of ancient-data.org.
Shebanq is a search engine for the Hebrew Bible, powered by the ETCBC4 linguistic database, formerly know as WIVU.
The data is archived for open access, clicking the Sources menu or the DANS logo at the bottom will bring you to the treasure trove.
Shebanq is open source. It consists of a webservice shemdros and a web application shebanq.

{{=T('License and Re-use')}}

The creators of the linguistic annotations that drive SHEBANQ, the ETCBC, want to facilitate optimal access to their data by researchers. All operations that are needed for the scholarly process are allowed. See the menu Sources for the locations of the data and the code. You are allowed to download the data and the code, build your own workflows on top of the data and even to redistribute your versions of the data and the code. The licence is:
This work is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 International License. That means:
  • You may not use the data for commercial applications;
  • You must give proper attribution to the data when you use it in new applications, preferably by citing its persistent identifier urn:nbn:nl:ui:13-048i-71
The ETCBC prefers to stress the potential of using its data, rather than the legal restrictions in doing so. It has published data, tools and documentation online that enables teams of biblical scholars and developers to reproduce all data processing that is needed for SHEBANQ. The recommended tool to start with is {{=A('laf-fabric', _href='https://github.com/ETCBC/laf-fabric', _target='_blank')}}. Its documentation describes in detail how to download the data and set up data-processing workflows. The ETCBC welcomes contributions that improve and enrich the interface of SHEBANQ. We are also open to implement mutual deep linking with other biblical data sources.
